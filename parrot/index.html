Parrotforce

Parrotforce provides executives,founders, and professionals with a 24/7 personal assistant service to help them manage their time more effectively
Visit Website

01

Project Vision

The Problem

Parrotforce was using a combination of different services and manual spreadsheets for their virtual assistant workflow. A slew of different apps for communication, customer management and employee tracking was a massive bottleneck for efficiency and growth.

Customer Overload

Parrotforce grew rapidly because of its unique offerings and superior customer support strategies. Now, they were at the limit of what their pieced together infrastracture could handle.

The Request

Parrotforce approached simublade to help create a solution that would combine some of the apps they were using to create a smoother experience for their users. Ultimately, Simublade built a comprehensive platform to manage the full customer experience.



Parrotforce also provided design and branding work for Parrotforce, developing a new logo and brand for the company. This case study will focus on the software developemnet portion of our work with Parrotforce.

02

Solution

Intelligent Routing System

Simublade created a solution that intelligent routes requests from a multiple of communication channels (calls, messages, emails and even Trello boards) directly to dynamically assigned personal assistants. The routing system quickly identifies the correct resource based on a variety of factors and connects them with the customers request for a seamless customer experience. Of course, popular services change from time to time, so the platform is extensible to utilize new services that have API access.

Conversational AI

It doesn't stop there though- the platform learns form customer requests and responses to improve experience over time. Using machine learning and natural language processing (NLP) the Parrotforce platform identifies and stores important pieces of conversational data to allow VAs to create an immersive experienc for clients.
